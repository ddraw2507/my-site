Who I Am

I grew up in a rural corner of western Nghệ An—where summer heat crackles in the air and the dry Lào winds seem to pull the moisture out of everything, even people. On nights when the power grid couldn’t keep up and electricity was cut, we would carry our mats out into the yard. Under the open sky, I’d fall asleep to the slow rhythm of a handmade palm-leaf fan—my grandmother’s craft—waved gently by my mother, by my grandmother. Looking back, I realize how lucky I was to have lived those moments. They remind me of where I come from, of the days when life was simpler and harder, and of the people I will always find my way back to.

To be honest, I don’t have a clear narrative for how I got from there to here. It’s not a story of overcoming or of transformation. It’s more like a series of small pivots, each one leading to the next, and all of them together shaping the person I am today.
