Who I Am

Born in the mid-nineties, raised in the north of Vietnam, still living there. An infrastructure engineer by training — which is a polite way of saying I draw bridges, roads, and tunnels for a living. Outside of work I keep small rituals: long walks, longer dinners, and the kind of conversation that leaves no residue.

A short ledger of the moments that shifted something. A first degree. A first real job. The morning I was asked to lead a team of one, which became a team of seven. iii.

The Work

I lead a small BIM team in Hanoi, serving clients across Europe — Italy mostly, but also Germany, France, Spain, the UK. We grew from one person to seven in three years, by taking careful work seriously and trusting each other. My evenings go to writing AutoCAD plugins in C# under the house name DDRAW: small tools that save my team hours and, occasionally, bring a little delight to a tedious afternoon.

Places, Walked

Italy, in the summer of 2025: a working trip that turned into a wandering one. Avellino and Ferrara for the offices; then south to Rome, west to Pisa and Florence, and north to the Dolomites, where the Geisler Alm looked back at me like a wall of prayer. A notebook of photographs, detours, small meals, and recommendations for the next traveler.
