Journal · June - July 2024 · Central Vietnam

Travelling alone for two weeks

My summer trip in 2024 holds some of the most memorable moments of my life - a journey I truly needed to heal my soul. Earlier that year, I lost my grandmother, the person who had always loved and cared for me since I was a little child. I still remember her sweet jackfruit fiber soup, her cool and refreshing black bean dessert, those summer afternoons when she fanned me with a fan made from areca palm leaves, and how she always reminded me to lock the door whenever I went out.

As a kid, I found it all annoying - everything she did for me felt so obvious, so taken for granted. Only when I grew up did I realize how immense her love was for me, her youngest grandchild. She was 99 that year. Just one more year, and my father's hardworking mother would have turned a hundred - something that, when you think about it, fills you with such deep regret. On top of that, a breakup after just two months shattered my faith in love. I'll simply say we "weren't meant to be," but deep down, I carried wounds that were hard to heal.

Nha Trang — with family

I took two weeks off work, starting with a trip to Nha Trang with my parents. I was so happy to fulfill my goal of taking them on a vacation at least once a year. After that came a string of days wandering from Nha Trang to Phú Yên, Huế, Đà Nẵng, and Hội An.

The sea in Nha Trang was stunning - the water so crystal clear I could hardly believe my eyes. Three days of relaxation there with my parents, and it felt absolutely complete.

Phú Yên — where healing began

After saying goodbye to my parents - they headed back to Đà Nẵng to visit my niece - I caught a bus to Phú Yên the very next morning. The sweltering Central Vietnam summer heat wore me out a little. But you know what? My first day in Phú Yên swept all that tiredness away. I arrived at An Hải fishing village, grabbed an enormous cup of sugarcane juice for just 10,000 VND, hopped on a speedboat to Cù Lao Mái Nhà island, and spent the entire day taking in its untouched beauty.

As the afternoon faded, I paddled a SUP board out to the middle of the sea and just lay there. I breathed deeply, letting the waves carry me adrift for a whole hour. It was a feeling I had longed for over such a long time, ever since I graduated and started working. The sunset that evening was pure magic - I had never seen anything so beautiful. I was being healed.

When night fell, I pitched my tent right by the beach, grabbed some food, and joined a group of young travelers nearby for a card game. Chatting with strangers, laughing with them the very first time you meet - that is the special thing about solo trips.

The next morning, I woke up at 4:30, quickly made my way through the forest to the far side of the island, carefully set up my camera on a tripod, and savored the sunrise at the easternmost point of my country - slowly, blissfully, and all alone.
